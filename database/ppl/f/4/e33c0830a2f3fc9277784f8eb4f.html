SYDNEY MORNING HERALD - - - - SHIPPING ARRIVALS - - - - Page 4.
January 12th, 1855 - "Royal Saxon", barque, 450 tons, Captain N. Jackson, from London 1st September. Passengers - Mr. Maitland & son, Miss Toogood, & Mr.Shettle. Agent R.Towns.
- - - IMPORTS - - - -Lots of Packages for lots of people etc; 3002 deals, 35 tons of coal, 285 packages, by Order.
The "Royal Saxon" has made a long passage from London, having met with light variable winds the greater part of the voyage. On the 15th September spoke the "Marion", from Liverpool to Adelaide, & on the 23rd December spoke the "Rienzi", from London for same port, 98 days out. Mrs. Maitland, a passenger, died on the 17th November, after a long illness. On the 24th same month a seaman named Margot, was lost overboard from the forecastle during a gale, & the ship was rolling heavily.
