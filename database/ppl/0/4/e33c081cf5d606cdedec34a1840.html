Narrative

1995 - Lieut. Col., "D" Coy.,13th Batt.; b. Sydney 23/8/1893; educ. Auburn P.S.; engineer; enlisted 4/10/1914; emb. 23/12/1914. War Service: Egypt; Gallipoli (Landing); at Quinn's Post he conducted his platoon safely out of a tight corner, and was awarded a second star; he was serverely wounded in the shoulder at Lone Pine, 7/8/1915; recovered; rejoined Coy. in Egypt Jan 1916, & when the 45th Batt. was being formed he was given the rank of Capt.,& charge of "A" Coy., 45th Batt., left Egypt for France March 1916, & was again severely wounded in the Somme campaign while in the front trenches, cheering his men during the awful bombardment on August 14th; for his bravery he was awarded the Military Cross, & in Sept. was made Major; on his recovery in November he was boarded for home, but refused to go; rejoined unit 1/11/1916; was then given temporary command, & later was made Lieut.-Col. in charged of the 46th Batt., but after a few months was given command of his old battalion, the 45th. Lieut.-Col. Perry was also mentioned in despatches. Prior to enlisting he was Area Officer at Auburn, & had been for eight years in the Engineering School at the Sydney University.
