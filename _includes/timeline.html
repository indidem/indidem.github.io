Timeline

Key research and project milestones.

  • 1980-1985

    First sparks

    Shirley's interest in family history was sparked in the early 1980s.

  • 1985-2010

    Evolving research methods

    In the early years much of Shirley's research was personally, manually collected from parish records, cemeteries, local libraries, community historical groups and genealogy networks. The introduction of the internet brought improvements in correspondence, by email and through online genealogy networks, plus as genealogical records were digitised around the world, opportunities to better identify and connect individuals.

  • 1995-today

    Reporting on the puzzle

    Not long after starting her research, Shirley began assembling and distributing book-length reports on a range of significant family members. As part of that process, Shirley explored several database systems, and ultimately developed the current data set using the Personal Ancestral File software, which is now discontinued.

  • December 2019

    Shirley's 90th birthday celebration

    Shirley's approaching birthday has provided the inspiration for reviewing the research to date. We hope that reassessing the research, and continuing Shirley's publishing goals, in new formats, will help the work persist into the future.
